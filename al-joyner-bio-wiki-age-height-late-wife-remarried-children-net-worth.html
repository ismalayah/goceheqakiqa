Al Joyner Athlete

Joyner is a vastly recognized track and field coach and former athlete. Currently, he coaches and directs the Flo Jo Community Empowerment Foundation and Final Kick Marketing Group. Likewise, he is the 1984 Olympic gold medalist in the triple jump.

Prior, Al served as the coach of his late four-time Olympic medalist Florence Griffith Joyner. Citing his career, he was a three-time NCAA All-American indoor champion. He was also a three-time NCAA All-American and outdoor champion.

Furthermore, Joyner is a four-time Southland Conference champ. In addition, he had placed 8th in the triple jump at the World Championships in Helsinki, Finland. Afterward, he became the first African American in 80 years to win a gold medal in the triple jump.

Later, Al was honored with the Jim Thorpe Award as the best American competitor in an Olympic Field Event. Throughout, he has been inducted into the Arkansas State University Track and Field Hall of Fame.

Most noteworthy, Joyner was hired by SportsToday.com. While here, he wrote columns on track and field for the 2000 Olympic Games in Sydney, Australia. He also coached two athletes with their sights on the 2000 U.S. Olympic Track and Field team.

On the other hand, Al served as the sprint & jump coach for the 2005 USOC Paralympics team in Helsinki. He then joined the USATF/ USOC coaching staff and became the full-time USOC high-performance jump coach in 2007.

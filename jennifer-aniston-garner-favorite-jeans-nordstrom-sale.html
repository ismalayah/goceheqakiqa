Celeb-Loved Jeans Are Majorly Marked Down at a Nordstrom Sale

 Posted on May 16, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  366 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Hard pants might just be back. At least that's what Nordstrom wants us to think. While the retailer's major Winter Sale is over, some of the best deals are only just beginning. And some of those major secret post-sale deals are on, you guessed it, denim.

But not just any kind of denim, the kind of denim the most famous Jennifers of the world swear by. We're talking about none other than Jennifer Garner and Jennifer Aniston. Both Jens often opt for more practical outfits made up of wardrobe essentials. It's what makes them so lovable, and now some of their go-to pieces are more attainable than ever thanks to Nordstrom's post-Winter Sale markdowns.

Jennifer Aniston loves Rag & Bone jeans, and other celebrities like Katie Holmes have been catching on. If you haven't invested in a pair of Aniston's famous go-to denim, now is finally your chance. There's even a pair of Rag & Bone high-rise slim fit jeans for $90, a looser option for $102, and a pair that's basically denim leggings for $90. And while Rag & Bone has gone on sale before, these are absolutely some of the best prices we've seen in months. Designer denim worn by Jennifer Aniston for under $100? Count us in.

As for Jennifer Garner, she swears by Joe's Jeans, another denim brand that'll often cost you a minimum of $198. That's pretty steep for jeans, but now there are a handful of Garner-approved pairs for $100. Garner also hasn't given up hope on skinny jeans like the rest of the internet, and if you're willing to try out the controversial look, a pair by Joe's Jeans are now under $125. If you'd like to stick to what's less contentious, there's a pair of wide-leg jeans everyone can get behind for $119.

The best thing about both of these denim brands being on sale is that you can buy one pair from each and then channel both Aniston and Garner. We're calling it: 2022 is the year of the hard pants return. Leggings are so 2020 anyway.
